ITEM 1. BUSINESS
ACME Robotics Inc. ("ACME", "the Company") designs, manufactures, and sells autonomous
warehouse robotics systems for logistics and e-commerce fulfillment customers. The Company
was founded in 2014 and is headquartered in Austin, Texas. As of fiscal year end, ACME had
approximately 2,300 full-time employees and generated revenue of $1.42 billion, up 18% year
over year. ACME's primary product lines are the AutoPick fulfillment arm, the FleetNav
autonomous mobile robot platform, and the WarehouseOS software suite.

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS
Market Concentration Risk. Approximately 34% of ACME's fiscal year 2025 revenue was
generated from its top five customers. The loss of any of these customers, or a significant
reduction in their orders, could materially and adversely affect the Company's revenue and
results of operations.

Supply Chain Risk. ACME relies on a limited number of suppliers for critical robotics
components, including precision actuators and lidar sensors. Approximately 60% of lidar
sensor units were sourced from a single supplier in fiscal year 2025. Disruption to this
supplier relationship could delay production and shipment of the Company's products.

Regulatory Risk. The Company's autonomous mobile robots are subject to an evolving
patchwork of state and local workplace safety regulations. Changes to these regulations,
or the introduction of new regulations governing autonomous equipment in shared workspaces,
could increase compliance costs or restrict deployment of the Company's products in certain
jurisdictions.

Competition Risk. The autonomous warehouse robotics market is highly competitive. The
Company competes with both established industrial automation companies and well-funded
venture-backed startups. Some competitors have greater financial, technical, and marketing
resources than ACME.

Cybersecurity Risk. ACME's products connect to customer warehouse management systems and
collect operational data. A security breach affecting the Company's products or cloud
infrastructure could result in unauthorized access to customer data, reputational harm, and
potential regulatory penalties.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
Total revenue for fiscal year 2025 was $1.42 billion, compared to $1.20 billion in fiscal
year 2024, an increase of 18.3%. Gross margin improved to 41.2% from 38.7% in the prior
year, driven primarily by manufacturing efficiencies in the FleetNav product line. Research
and development expenses increased to $187 million, representing 13.2% of revenue, as the
Company continued to invest in next-generation navigation software. The Company's market
capitalization as of fiscal year end was approximately $6.8 billion.

Liquidity and Capital Resources. As of fiscal year end, ACME held $412 million in cash and
cash equivalents and had $150 million of outstanding long-term debt. The Company believes
its existing cash balance, together with cash generated from operations, will be sufficient
to fund operations for at least the next twelve months.
