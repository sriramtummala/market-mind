# Internal Trading & Compliance Policy Handbook (Synthetic Demo Version)
This document is a synthetic stand-in for internal broker-dealer trading policies and
FINRA/SEC-style compliance rules, written for demo/training purposes only.

## Policy 1 — Minimum Market Capitalization for Unsupervised Trade Execution
Trades in equities with a market capitalization below $1 billion require mandatory
compliance officer sign-off before execution. Trades in equities with a market
capitalization below $500 million additionally require a documented suitability review.

## Policy 2 — Going Concern Flag
Any security whose most recent 10-K discloses a "going concern" risk, an accumulated
deficit exceeding $500 million, or management's own disclosure that existing cash will not
fund operations for the next twelve months must be flagged as HIGH RISK and requires
compliance review before any new client position is opened.

## Policy 3 — Concentration Limits
No single position may exceed 25% of a client's total portfolio value without documented
client acknowledgment of concentration risk. Positions in securities where the issuer
itself discloses customer or revenue concentration above 30% require an additional risk
disclosure to the client.

## Policy 4 — Restricted / Watch List
Trades in any issuer currently on the firm's restricted list are prohibited without written
approval from the Chief Compliance Officer. Trades in issuers on the watch list require
supervisory pre-clearance.

## Policy 5 — Pattern Day Trading & Margin
Clients flagged as Pattern Day Traders must maintain minimum equity of $25,000 in their
margin account. Trades that would bring an account below this threshold require a margin
call notification before execution.

## Policy 6 — Material Non-Public Information (MNPI) Controls
Employees and automated systems must not execute or recommend trades based on information
that is not publicly disclosed via SEC filings, press releases, or other public channels.
Any AI-generated trade recommendation must cite only publicly available sources.

## Policy 7 — Suitability Review for High-Risk Sectors
Recommendations involving clinical-stage biotechnology issuers with no approved products
and negative net income must include a documented suitability review confirming the
client's risk tolerance and investment time horizon support a high-risk, speculative
position.

## Policy 8 — Human-in-the-Loop Requirement
No AI-generated trade recommendation may be executed automatically. All AI-generated trade
recommendations must be presented to a licensed representative or the client for explicit
approval before any order is placed.
